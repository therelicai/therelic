  confusingly similar name, including subdomains, brand-adjacent URLs,

- **Selling merchandise** (apparel, stickers, swag) bearing the marks.

## Naming a fork

If you maintain a fork that diverges materially from upstream, please
choose a distinct name and make the relationship to upstream clear:

- ✅ "Acme Mediator (a fork of The Relic)"
- ✅ "Acme Mediator, originally based on The Relic"
- ❌ "Acme Relic"
- ❌ "The Relic by Acme"
- ❌ "TheRelic Pro"

You are still required by the Apache License to retain copyright,
patent, trademark, and attribution notices from the upstream source.

## Requesting permission

Email **trademarks@therelic.dev** with:

1. The mark you want to use.
2. The proposed use, with mockups or examples.
3. Whether the use is commercial.
4. Your timeline.

We try to respond within 10 business days. Permission, when granted, is
typically informal and revocable; a written trademark license can be
arranged for ongoing commercial uses.

## Why this exists

This policy is here to protect users and adopters of The Relic from
confusion about who is shipping what. We want broad adoption of the
software (that's why it's Apache 2.0). We do not want third parties
shipping unmaintained, malicious, or substantially modified code under
the same name and damaging the trust users place in The Relic.

These are the same rules followed by Apache Software Foundation
projects (Kafka, Cassandra, Solr, etc.), the Linux Foundation projects
(Kubernetes, etcd, Prometheus, etc.), and similar trademark-aware open
source projects.

If anything here is unclear, ask. The intent is openness with brand
integrity, not obstruction.
